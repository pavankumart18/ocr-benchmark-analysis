LOW-YIELD CIGARETTES

A RESEARCH PROPOSAL BY THE AMERICAN HEALTH FOUNDATION

“IN DRAFT”

September 23, 1982


INTRODUCTION

Recent preliminary studies by the American Health Foun-
dation indicate that smokers who switch to a low-yield
cigarette with air-channels in the filter tip can experience
a markedly high uptake of smoke. This is reflected in a
higher-than-expected nicotine-cotinine level in the blood as
well as in a measurably increased pulse rate (1,2). How-
ever, the study was limited to dosimetry in 4 subjects who
shifted from their preferred low-yield cigarette (<0.6 mg
nicotine/cig) to the special filter cigarette (cigarette B:
0.94 mg tar, 0.13 mg nicotine, 1.1 mg CO) or to an ordinary
filter tipped cigarette yielding 0.86 mg tar, 0.12 mg nico-
tine, and 1.4 mg CO under standardised machine smoking con-
ditions.
