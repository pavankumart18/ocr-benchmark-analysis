UPTAKE OF SMOKE BY INHALEES UPON CHANGING TO LOW-YIELD CIGARETTES

A RESEARCH PROPOSAL BY THE AMERICAN HEALTH FOUNDATION
1ST DRAFT
September 28, 1982

INTRODUCTION

Recent preliminary studies by the American Health Foundation indicate that smokers who switch to a low-yield cigarette with air-channels in the filter tip can experience a relatively high uptake of smoke. This is reflected in a higher-than-expected nicotine-cotinine level in the blood as well as in a measurably increased puls rate (1,2). However, the study was limited to dosimetry in 4 subjects who shifted from their preferred low-yield cigarette (<0.6 mg nicotine/cig) to the special filter cigarette (Cigarette 8; 0.94 mg tar, 0.13 mg nicotine, 1.1 mg CO) or to an ordinary filter tipped cigarette yielding 0.86 mg tar, 0.12 mg nicotine, and 1.4 mg CO under standardized machine smoking conditions.
